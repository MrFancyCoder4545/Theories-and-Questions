Why do we cry?

This is a very important question to me. I watched this video about a girl whose struggling father does all of these things that others see as bad and criminalistic just to support her. I thought to myself after seeing that video and it made me ask myself, why did I cry at that video? Why did that video in specific reach so far into my mind as to make me cry? All humans cry, whether it be a boy or girl, someone tough or someone a little more sensitive, or someone young or someone old; the reason why this question is so hard to answer is that we all cry for different reasons. To answer this question, I have to look at the world from not only my eyes, but everyone else's. For example, I cried at that video because the girl realizes that her father is endeavoring all of the pain and struggle because she's his daughter. I cried for the father, how he has a daughter that's counting on him for survival and he is taking on such a huge debt and suffering so he can give his daughter a future, a future unlike his own. The point being, crying is something that very few people realize as the most beautiful form of sadness anybody could experience. So, my theory on why we all cry is that we have the beautiful and scary ability to feel what others are feeling, but that's called empathy, and that's just human nature. We can't escape it, so we have to embrace it and use whatever sad or depressing experiences to help us see what's right in front of us everyday, a pretty good life. Tell me what you think about this question because I would really like to have different perspectives on this. Also, please tell me what other questions you want me to answer in the future.

What is the difference between democrats and republicans?

The US has prided itself on its unique society and government for over 200 years. One major part of our government is our two major political parties: democratic and republican. These two parties exhibit little apparent differentiality with today's modernized government. Though, the two parties were derived from two older parties, the Federalists and the Democratic-Republicans. The Democratic-Republican party eventually became the Republican party, and the Federalist party became the Northern and Southern Democratic Party; the two parties merged afterwards into the modern Democratic party. Anyways, the Federalists wanted a strong central government and based its priorities on protecting America's budding industries. The Democratic-Republicans, or Anti-Federalists, believed that the federal government should leave them alone as much as possible; they wanted most of the power to fall in the hands of individual cities and states. The Federalists' ideals were passed onto the Democrats and the Democratic Republicans passed their ideals onto the Republicans.

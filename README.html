What is the difference between democrats and republicans?

The US has prided itself on its unique society and government for over 200 years. One major part of our government is our two major political parties: democratic and republican. These two parties exhibit little apparent differentiality with today's modernized government. Though, the two parties were derived from two older parties, the Federalists and the Democratic-Republicans. The Democratic-Republican party eventually became the Republican party, and the Federalist party became the Northern and Southern Democratic Party; the two parties merged afterwards into the modern Democratic party. Anyways, the Federalists wanted a strong central government and based its priorities on protecting America's budding industries. The Democratic-Republicans, or Anti-Federalists, believed that the federal government should leave them alone as much as possible; they wanted most of the power to fall in the hands of individual cities and states. The Federalists' ideals were passed onto the Democrats and the Democratic Republicans passed their ideals onto the Republicans. Sometimes for the better, instilling morals, and in some cases, helping instill facts for a quiz or test in a catchy and easily remembered way. My theory is that music gives an upbeat and jolly way of telling a story, but in today's modern world, people focus on the rhythmic part of music. I believe that people find the steady beat catchy, therefore creating interest in it. The beat stimulates emotions, like happiness, sadness, or even anger. Why are there so many languages?

Since the development of civilization, people have been using language to communicate, and when they started venturing other continents, they kept their languages. If different people from different continents, why wouldn't they make a universal language to communicate? My theory on why they did this is because it would have taken too long to translate everything between the languages and to get the rest of the world to comply.
